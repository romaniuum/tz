What's happening in Washington

Mark your calendars for the 2025 Discover Pass Free Days

On free days, no Discover Pass is required for parking at state parks or on lands managed by the Departments of Natural Resources and Fish and Wildlife.

New law now in effect to help protect endangered orca population

As of Jan. 1, 2025, boaters in Washington waters must stay at least 1,000 yards away from Southern Resident orcas, the smallest of four killer whale communities in the northeast Pacific Ocean.

Department of Health launched new winter seasonal hazards dashboard

The dashboard aims to help state residents prepare for winter health dangers by providing daily-updated data on severe weather, motor vehicle crashes, falls, and cold-related illnesses.

Washington sends firefighters and first responders to support California wildfire response

The Emergency Management Division is coordinating 45 fire engines, 11 trucks, and 146 firefighters from 43 fire departments across the state to help in the crisis.

Visit our State Capitol during the 2025 legislative session

This year’s session runs from Jan. 13 to April 27 and is one of the most exciting times to be on the Capitol Campus. Find out what you need to know before you visit.

Prepare your home for flood

Relentless fall and winter rains mean that flood risk is on the rise. Make sure your home is ready if there is imminent flooding in your area.

Looking for SecureAccess Washington (SAW)?

SecureAccess Washington is a tool to get access to all online state government services in one place. Use a single login to access services including licensing, employment security, and paid leave.
